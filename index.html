1.0 – Course Introduction

This module introduces the structure and expectations of the Sick Call Screener Course.

1.1 – Scope of Care

The Sick Call Screener Program exists to provide timely, high‑quality care for active‑duty personnel with minor medical conditions. Sick Call Screeners (SCS) do not function as independent providers. They operate under the direct supervision of a Medical Officer (MO), Physician Assistant (PA), Nurse Practitioner (NP), or Independent Duty Corpsman (IDC).

Patient Flow & Sick Call Operations

  • Patients check in at the front desk and present a valid DoD ID card.
  • Clerks log the visit according to local SOP and retrieve the medical record.
  • Patients are directed to the waiting or screening area and are generally seen in order of arrival.
  • Personnel must be able to identify patients requiring immediate care and notify the supervising provider without delay.
  • Routine patients receive vital signs and an initial complaint review by qualified enlisted medical personnel.

Screener Responsibilities

  • Conduct patient interviews and follow approved procedures.
  • Recognize personal limitations and seek assistance whenever doubt exists.
  • Patients may request to be seen by an MO/PA/NP/IDC at any time.

Scope of Care Guidelines

  • Use the SOAP format for every patient encounter.
  • Consult the supervising provider before the patient leaves the treatment facility.
  • 100% of SCS records must be reviewed and countersigned by the supervising provider.
  • Screeners may order labs only when allowed by local policy; all others require provider approval.
  • Screeners may evaluate and treat only minor illnesses with approved protocols and direct supervision.

Minor Illnesses Screeners May Treat

  • Minor headaches
  • Uncomplicated upper respiratory infections
  • Uncomplicated ENT conditions
  • Minor dermatological conditions
  • Minor musculoskeletal problems
  • Uncomplicated gastrointestinal conditions
  • Minor wounds not requiring suturing or complex care
  • Hypertension (serial BP checks only, when ordered)
  • Suture removal
  • Staple removal

Assisting With Conditions Outside Scope

Screeners may assist the medical provider with conditions beyond their scope, including:

  • General HEENT, respiratory, cardiovascular, GI, neurological, musculoskeletal, dermatological, endocrine, and environmental complaints
  • Mental health concerns
  • Any situation where only history and basic physical exam can be obtained

Immediate Referral Criteria

The following require immediate referral to an MO/PA/NP/IDC:

  • Fever ≥ 103°F, or >100.4°F for 48 hours
  • Respiratory rate > 28/min without cause
  • Pulse > 120/min without cause
  • Persistent diastolic BP > 105 mmHg over 3 days
  • Suspected infectious diseases (e.g., chickenpox, TB)
  • Chest pain of possible cardiac origin
  • Abdominal pain with fever or worsening symptoms
  • Bleeding, hematemesis, hemoptysis, hematochezia
  • Sudden testicular pain (possible torsion)
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Any degree of airway compromise
  • Return visits for unresolved complaints
  • Eye injuries, limb loss, significant visual changes
  • Altered mental status with suicidal/homicidal ideation
  • Loss of peripheral pulse
  • Tendon, nerve, or vessel damage
  • Capillary refill > 3 seconds with acute injury
  • Pain > 7/10
  • Open fractures, amputations, deformities, joint penetration

Referral to Higher Level of Care

  • Dermatitis, rashes, skin irritations
  • Vaccinations
  • UTIs, bladder infections, yeast infections
  • Ear infections
  • Diagnostic labs/tests
  • Physicals and wellness exams
  • Health screenings
  • Medication refills
  • Abnormal vital signs
  • Signs of infection (redness, pus)
  • Contaminated wounds
  • Animal/human bites
  • Puncture wounds
  • Any condition outside SCS comfort or protocol

Key Principles

  • This course does not train independent providers.
  • Screeners must always work under MO/PA/NP/IDC supervision.
