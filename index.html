Unit 1 – Scope of Care

1.0 – Course Introduction

This topic orients you to the Sick Call Screener Course (SCSC), how it is structured, and what is expected of you as a screener.

Overview & Learning Objectives

  • Describe the purpose of the Sick Call Screener Course.
  • Explain how Unit 1 foundations support Unit 2 systems‑based topics.
  • State your role and responsibilities as a sick call screener.

1.1 – Scope of Care

Scope of care defines what you are trained, authorized, and expected to do as a sick call screener. It sets the boundaries for your assessments, interventions, and decisions about escalation.

Key Learning Points

  • Scope is defined by training, policy, supervision, and regulations.
  • Recognize when a complaint exceeds your scope and requires escalation.
  • Red‑flag findings are triggers for referral, not independent management.
  • Practicing outside scope endangers patients and has legal consequences.

1.2 – Ethics

This topic covers ethical principles relevant to sick call screening, including confidentiality, respect for persons, and integrity in documentation and decision‑making.

1.3 – SOAP Note

SOAP notes provide a structured way to document patient encounters: Subjective, Objective, Assessment, and Plan.

  • Subjective – chief complaint, history, and relevant review of systems.
  • Objective – vital signs, focused exam, and tests.
  • Assessment – working diagnosis or differential.
  • Plan – treatment, education, follow‑up, and escalation.

1.4 – Patient Presentation

Patient presentation focuses on how you verbally communicate your findings to a supervising provider in a concise, organized way.

Unit 2 – Sick Call Screener

2.1 – Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose & Throat (HEENT)

This topic covers focused HEENT assessment for common sick call complaints such as sore throat, ear pain, sinus symptoms, eye irritation, and headache.

Key Learning Points

  • Clarify onset, duration, severity, and associated symptoms.
  • Perform a systematic HEENT exam as trained.
  • Recognize red‑flag findings that require escalation.
  • Differentiate benign conditions from those needing urgent referral.

2.2 – Respiratory System

Focused assessment of respiratory complaints, including cough and shortness of breath.

2.3 – Cardiovascular System

Evaluation of chest pain, palpitations, and syncope with attention to life‑threatening causes.

2.4 – Gastrointestinal System

Assessment of abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and other GI complaints.

2.5 – Genitourinary System

Focused GU history and exam for urinary and genital complaints.

2.6 – Neurological System

Evaluation of headaches, dizziness, weakness, and other neurologic symptoms.

2.7 – Musculoskeletal System – Upper Extremities

Assessment of upper extremity pain and injury.

2.8 – Musculoskeletal System – Neck and Spine

Focused evaluation of neck and spine complaints, including trauma and neurologic involvement.

2.9 – Musculoskeletal System – Lower Extremities

Assessment of lower extremity pain, injury, and gait issues.

2.10 – Integumentary System

Evaluation of rashes, wounds, and skin lesions.

2.11 – Endocrine System

Recognition of common endocrine‑related presentations, such as diabetes‑related complaints.

2.12 – Environmental Illness and Injuries

Assessment of heat and cold injuries, bites, stings, and other environmental exposures.

2.13 – Pharmacology / Medical Therapeutics

Safe use of medications within standing orders, including indications and contraindications.
